(March 6, 2024. Author Dave)

 

Outsider MC National Run 2024. Another fantastic day with a poker run ride around Cebu and Mactan Island to Mactan Clubhouse for lunch, then to a bar for further activities....Again thanks to Outsider MC Mactan Chapter for hosting the 2024 National Run.

 

(Total Ride: Angeles City -> Masbate -> Cebu -> Poker Run Mactan -> Cebu -> Negros -> Panay -> Mindoro -> Angeles City)

 

Total distance rode was 1728km. We took 6 ferry rides between islands and spent P32k ($572) (Â£450) on Gas, Hotels, Ferries and Food, plus P10k for the 2 days national run days for food, drinks and hotel. I think its time for a cold beer.....

 

Points to concider if traveling by bike around the Philippines based on our recent 7 day trip.

 

1 - BIKE

A. Ensure your bike is in good running road worthy condition and that it has a good suspension, lights and tires.

B. Ensure you have a tire repair kit and air pump with you and a few basic tools.

C. Never take a cooler bag with you for drinks as 7/11 stores are everywhere and the cooler bag just takes up unnecessary room on the bike.

 

2 - ROAD CONDITIONS

A. Never ride at night unless it's completely nessacery.

B. Very few on coming Vehicles at night will dip there headlights and you will be blinded.

C. The roads are pothole heaven in places, especially on main roads that trucks and buses ply.

D. Especially on twisty roads pay close attention to wet patches in corners it could be oil or diesel from a truck or jeepney.

E. Be very aware in the Provinces tricycles are the means of most transportation, and they have total disregard for other vehicles on the road.

F. If riding at night or early morning expect to encounter small motorcycles and tricycles which have no lights whatsoever.

G. Mostly early morning, around sunrise, be very aware of dogs, chickens and ducks on the road or crossing the roads. Dogs and Chickens will bolt across the road in front of you, but if you happen to encounter a duck or ducks in the road they just walk across the road, they don't give a F***.

 

3 - FERRIES

A. Never believe what it tells you about ferry schedules online.

B. The only ferry company that sticks to somewhat of a time schedule is Fastcat Ferries.

C. Most other ferry companies in places have no fixed schedules and they leave whenever they have enough cargo or passengers.

D. Food on ferries is limited, with the exception of Fastcat Ferries, they have a wide range of sandwiches on board.

E. Toilet facilities onboard Ferries are unclean, especially if #2 is required.

F. Some ferry services serve beer onboard some don't.

G. Don't be supprised if the bunk bed you have been assigned on your ticket is taken over by a rude Filipino who thinks he has the right to it.

H. Most Ferries will lash or tie you bike down, usually with rope. It advisable to carry your own small cargo strap and tie your own bike down, as they have no regard for your bikes paint work.

 

4 - PORT AUTHORITY & PAPER WORK REQUIRMENTS

A. Expect each port to be different in paper work requirements.

B. OR/CR is required in some ports but not in others.

C. Coast guard clearance, again is required in some ports but not others.

D. Government ID, again required in some ports but not all.

E. When processing the paper work expect to be given the run around, not all payments are made at the same window. Some requirements are outside the Port and different buildings, so expect to be frustrated at the lack of organisation.

F. If riding a big bike be prepared to pay a higher price for 400cc and above plus pay for rider on top of that.

 

5 - HEALTH

A. Carry a basic medical kit which incluides shit medicines, hydration salts, pain killers.

B. Drink plenty of fluids, that's why 7/11s are important, make regular stops.

C. Carry bog roll with you, most service stations have tabo tabo ass wash only.

D. Carry sun block or wear long sleeve shirts. You may need a long sleeve shirt for early morning rides as the temperature can get down to 20deg early in the morning in some places, even colder if riding in the mountains.

E. Ensure your in reasonable physical shape to be able to ride long distances. Your body takes a beating on these roads here.

 

6 - ACCOMMODATION

A. When planning a trip do not book hotels in advance or until you are on the ferry to that destination. Agoda and Booking.com can be booked on line while onboard ferry, but Internet signal can be bad at times, especially between islands.

B. Good Hotels to book at reasonable prices are Novo Hotels, Prince Express Inns, GO Hotels and Red Door, most under P1500/night.

 

7 - GAS STATIONS

A. Prices for gas can vary in difference from P10 or more depending on which gas station you use. We tried to use Petron most of the time, but sometimes we were forced to fill up at other stations where the gas prices were much higher.

B. Not all gas stations have toilet facilities and if they do most are unclean and very basic, so be prepared, carrying bog roll is a must.
